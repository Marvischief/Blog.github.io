Vulnerability: The Key to our Humanity

Ask a thousand people to kill a lion, and you'll get no takers. But offer a prize, and suddenly everyone's a lion killer. Why? Because humans are wired to seek rewards. We see a prize, and our brains convince us that we can do anything. We're willing to put ourselves in danger, just for a chance at a reward. We are willing to open ourselves up to vulnerability, but only if there is something in it for us. Yet, this same vulnerability that is the root of shame, insecurity, and setbacks is also the birthplace of joy, creativity, love, life, and a sense of belonging and worthiness. How much more do we need to see before we are willing to embrace vulnerability for all that it is?

We live in a vulnerable world but numb vulnerability - “Brené Brown”

We humans try to numb our vulnerability, but it's like trying to hold back the tide, This is a defence mechanism that we develop from a young age, when we learn that being vulnerable can make us feel exposed and weak. From the moment we're born, our alter-egos have taught us that vulnerability is weakness. From our personal lives to our professional careers, we all have moments where we are humbled by our experiences. These moments can be good or bad, and they can happen in any area of our lives, such as our relationships, our businesses, or our health. When we experience humility, it is often because we have been reminded of our limitations. We may have made a mistake, or we may have been faced with a challenge that we cannot overcome on our own. In these moments, we are forced to confront our own humanity and to admit that we are not perfect. However, humility can also be a positive experience. It can teach us to be more grateful for what we have, and it can make us more compassionate towards others. Sometimes, we are confronted with our own limitations in a way that forces us to humble ourselves. This can happen when we are sick, when we fail at something, or when we are simply faced with a situation that is beyond our control. We're all goal-getters, but even the best of us need to be humbled sometimes. Those moments are a reminder that we're human, and that's what makes life beautiful.

To acknowledge one's limitations is to demonstrate courage - “Criss Jami”

  • Courage to be imperfect
  • Compassion to be kind to ourselves first and then to others
  • Connection as a result of authenticity; willing to let go of the thoughts of what we should be and accepting who we are.

HOW DO WE NUMB VULNERABILITY?

  1. We humans are hardwired to seek certainty in an uncertain world :

    We do this by creating belief systems, such as religion and politics, that give us a sense of order and meaning.
    Religion has traditionally been a source of comfort and guidance for people, offering a framework for understanding the mysteries of life and death. However, in recent years, religion has become increasingly politicised, with different groups using it to justify their own beliefs and agendas.
    Politics, too, has become a forum for conflict and division. In the past, politicians were more likely to focus on finding common ground and working together to solve problems. Today, however, political discourse is often characterised by name-calling, finger-pointing, and blame-shifting.
    This trend towards certainty and division is a reflection of our own inner vulnerability. When we feel uncertain or threatened, we are more likely to cling to our beliefs and lash out at those who disagree with us. We need to find ways to embrace uncertainty and complexity, rather than trying to control them. We need to learn to listen to each other and find common ground, even when we disagree. And we need to find ways to express our pain and discomfort in a healthy way, rather than using it as an excuse to hurt others.

  2. We Perfect:

    I'm the kind of person who wants their life to be perfect. I mean, who doesn't? But it doesn't work! I mean, we literally take fat from our bums and put it in our cheeks! I hope in a hundred years people will look back and say, "Wow, those people were crazy! What were they thinking?" But then again, maybe I'm the crazy one. Maybe in a hundred years, everyone will be taking fat from their bums and putting it in their cheeks. Who knows?
    But for now, I'm just going to keep being me. I'm going to keep striving for perfection, even though I know it's impossible. And I'm going to keep laughing at myself, even when I make mistakes. Because at the end of the day, that's all we can do. We can just keep trying our best, and hope for the best. We live in a world where there is no such thing as perfection. We are all flawed, in our own way.

  3. We Pretend:

    We're so afraid of being judged, we've become like those crabs in a bucket, we walk around with our hearts in our pockets, hoping no one will notice. We act like our habits and actions don't affect anyone else, like our decisions don't matter. But the truth is, we're all connected. Everything we do has an impact, whether we see it or not. We see someone trying to climb out, and we pull them back down. We're so afraid of being left behind, we'd rather keep everyone else down with us. But what if we stopped judging each other so much? What if we started lifting each other up instead? what if we let those walls down? What if we let ourselves be seen, flaws and all? What if we lived our lives with courage and vulnerability? Why don't we love and live with our whole hearts, even though there's no guarantee of reward? Why don't we practice gratitude and joy, and believe that we are enough? What if we realised that we're all in this together?
    It would be scary, sure. It's scary to be vulnerable. It's scary to put yourself out there and risk getting hurt. But it's also the only way to truly live. it would also be amazing. We would finally be free to be ourselves, without fear of judgment. We would connect with others on a deeper level. We would experience the full range of human emotions, from joy to sorrow, from love to loss.

How pitiful it is that we numb our innate selves and become miserable. In our search for purpose and meaning, we're led astray by those who only want to use us, we're trapped in a cycle of abuse, we fill our stomachs with beer and live lazy, lonely lives. we’re trapped in a cycle that we can't seem to break.

"It's so sad that we numb our true selves and end up living miserable lives.”

So next time you're feeling scared, remember this: you are not alone. We're all in this together. And we're all enough.
